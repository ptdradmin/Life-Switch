🔒 Privacy Policy

Summary

Life Switch is built on a simple promise: your secrets belong only to you. We encrypt everything locally before it ever reaches our servers. We do not sell, share, or profit from your personal data.

1. Data We Collect

  • Account data: Email address or phone number (used for authentication only)
  • Profile data: Display name, language preference, timer settings
  • Vault content: Encrypted messages, encrypted file references — we cannot read these
  • Contact list: Names, emails, and phone numbers of your designated beneficiaries
  • Check-in timestamps: Date of your last Pulse button press
  • Device tokens: For push notifications (FCM), stored only with your permission

2. How We Use Your Data

  • To authenticate you securely via Firebase Auth
  • To store and synchronize your encrypted vault content
  • To send alerts and transmit your secrets to designated beneficiaries when the timer expires
  • To send push notifications for emergency alerts (if enabled)

3. Encryption

All vault content (messages, codes, files) is encrypted client-side using AES-256 before being sent to our servers. The encryption key is derived from your user ID. We have no technical ability to read your vault content.

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with any third party for commercial purposes. Data is only shared with:

  • Firebase (Google): Authentication, database, and storage infrastructure
  • Your designated beneficiaries: Only when your timer expires and you have not responded

5. Data Retention

Your data is retained as long as your account exists. You can permanently delete your account and all associated data at any time from within the app (Profile → Delete My Account).

6. Children's Privacy

Life Switch is not intended for users under 17 years of age. We do not knowingly collect data from minors. If you believe a minor has created an account, please contact us immediately.

7. International Users

Life Switch is GDPR-compliant. Users in the European Union have the right to access, rectify, and erase their data, as well as the right to data portability. See the Your Data Rights section below.
